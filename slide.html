The Challenge: FL over GEO Satellites

Federated Learning

Enables collaborative model training on decentralized data, enhancing privacy and reducing bandwidth needs. However, it faces challenges with client heterogeneity and communication overhead.

GEO Satellite Networks

Offer expansive, continuous global coverage, but their high altitude (~35,786 km) imposes significant barriers for FL: high propagation delays (>500 ms) and limited bandwidth.

The Research Gap

While FL over LEO satellites has been explored, optimization strategies tailored specifically for the unique constraints of GEO networks remain critically underexplored.

Problem Formulation

Objective

Select an optimal subset of K clients in each round to balance the competing goals of model accuracy and communication latency.

This is formulated as a multi-objective optimization problem, which is NP-hard (a variant of the 0-1 knapsack problem).

Unified Scoring Function (φᵢ)

  • âᵢ: Normalized Accuracy Contribution
  • bᵢ: Normalized Bandwidth

Proposed Method & Workflow

1

Metadata Collection

Clients report latest status (accuracy, bandwidth, delay) to the GEO server.

2

Client Selection via LP

Server solves the relaxed Linear Program to find optimal selection scores for all clients.

3

Targeted Dispatch

Selects top K clients based on scores and dispatches the global model only to them.

Experimental Setup

Dataset

MNIST, partitioned across N=10 clients with non-IID distributions (Dirichlet α=0.5).

GEO Network Model

Communication latency sampled uniformly from [100, 500] ms and fixed per client.

Selection & Training

Select K=4 clients per round for 50 rounds. Model is a simple CNN trained with FedAvg.

Baselines for Comparison

  • GREEDY: Selects top K clients by accuracy only.
  • RANDOM: Uniformly random selection.

Conclusion & Future Work

Conclusion

The proposed LP-based client selection method effectively addresses the high-latency challenge of FL in GEO networks. It achieves high accuracy comparable to baselines while significantly reducing communication latency, making it well-suited for latency-constrained environments.

Future Work

  • Expand evaluations to a larger number of clients (N).
  • Test on more realistic GEO network models and real-world testbeds.
  • Explore adaptive weighting strategies for the scoring function.
  • Integrate communication-efficient techniques like model quantization.
